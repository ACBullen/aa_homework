Baked Tortelini

  • description
  • ingredients
  • directions

Baked tortelini is a fairly modern spin on the classic dish of tortelini. The the tortelini is boiled as normal, but instead of just a classic sauce, such as a bechamel or light tomato sauce, we can build a kind of casserole around the pasta, with bacon and peas adding a little extra substance, and a crunchy topping made of bread crumbs and a bit of olive oil to add an extra textural layer to the dish. Read more about tortelini.

Ingredients

  • 8-12 oz tortelini(fresh or dried)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
  • 2 slices of bacon cut into small pieces
  • 1-2 shallots diced finely
  • 1/2 teaspoon of fresh thyme
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 1/2 cups of heavy whipping cream
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/8 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Put tortelini into a pot of boiling water. Cook per package instructions and then reserve in the pot with a little olive oil to keep them from sticking together.
  3. combine olive oil and bread crumbs in a bowl and set aside.
  4. Add bacon to a pan on medium-high heat and cook until brown and crispy and a goood bit of fat has rendered off. Add shallots and cook until lightly caramelized.
  5. Add thyme and garlic and cook until fragrant.
  6. Add flour to pan and allow about a minute to brown. Then whisk in the cream and chicken broth (Go slowly and stir constantly, the goal is a smooth sauce). Give it a minute at a simmer to thicken.
  7. Add parmesan off the heat and salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Combine tortelini, finished sauce, and peas in a 9x9 baking dish. Cover with breadcrumb mixture and place in the oven until the sauce is bubbly and the topping is browned (~15 minutes depending on your oven and how spread out your topping is).
  9. Serve in bowls with a nice glass of wine!
